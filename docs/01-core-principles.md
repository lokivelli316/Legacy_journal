# Core Principles

## 1. Preserve truth, not performance

The journal does not exist to prove productivity, intelligence, strength, or emotional depth. It exists to preserve a truthful human record.

## 2. Presence can be brief

Three honest sentences can be complete. Length is determined by what the day needs, not by guilt.

## 3. Missing days may remain missing

A gap is more truthful than invented continuity. A later reconstruction must be labeled as reconstruction.

## 4. Reality outranks the draft

A planned entry is a prompt, not evidence. The lived day may confirm, revise, replace, or invalidate it.

## 5. Ordinary life belongs in the archive

Not every entry needs a lesson, discovery, crisis, or polished ending.

## 6. Mistakes remain visible

Corrections may sit beside the original. Accountability comes before inspiration.

## 7. The reader is not responsible for the writer

A journal can share hard truths without transferring emotional custody to the person reading it.

## 8. AI assists; the human witnesses

AI can organize, challenge, compress, expand, compare, and ask questions. The human author decides what happened, what it meant, and what may be published.
