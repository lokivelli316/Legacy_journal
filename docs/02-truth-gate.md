# The Truth Gate

The Truth Gate prevents plans, interpretations, and reconstructions from quietly becoming facts.

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Observed** | What directly happened or was verified |
| **Felt** | What the writer genuinely experienced |
| **Interpreted** | What the writer currently thinks it meant |
| **Unknown** | What cannot yet be known responsibly |
| **Planned** | What was intended, not proof it occurred |
| **Reconstructed** | Written later from memory and labeled honestly |

## Example

**Observed:** I received no reply before the end of the day.

**Felt:** I felt ignored and became angry.

**Interpreted:** I initially assumed the silence was deliberate.

**Unknown:** I do not know why the person did not reply.

**Planned:** I intended to finish the conversation that evening.

**Reconstructed:** This account was written two days later from notes and memory.

The labels are not bureaucracy. They stop emotional certainty from impersonating evidence.
