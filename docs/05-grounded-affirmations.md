# Grounded Affirmations

Grounded affirmations use evidence instead of exaggeration.

## Governing rule

Do not force yourself to repeat a statement your nervous system immediately rejects. Move down the believability ladder until the statement becomes honest enough to carry.

## Believability ladder

1. **Direct:** I can repair this mistake.
2. **Learning:** I am learning how to repair mistakes without hiding them.
3. **Willingness:** I am willing to consider that this mistake does not define all of me.
4. **Permission:** I am allowed to pause before deciding what this event means about me.
5. **Tiny step:** For the next ten minutes, I only need to preserve the evidence and breathe.

## A grounded affirmation should

- tell the truth about the present
- use prior evidence
- give permission without removing responsibility
- ask only for the next humane action

## Avoid

- “I am unstoppable.”
- “Everything happens for a reason.”
- “I am healed.”
- “I will never repeat the pattern.”
- Any statement that denies pain, uncertainty, consequences, or biological limits
