# Beginner Use Cases

Start with a real-life situation, not a blank page.

Each section below includes a plain-language prompt you can paste into ChatGPT after completing the setup in [`USE_WITH_CHATGPT.md`](USE_WITH_CHATGPT.md).

## 1. Preserve one ordinary day

**Use when:** Nothing dramatic happened, but you want your family or future self to know what life actually felt like.

```text
Help me write a short Legacy Journal entry about today. Nothing major happened. Ask me for one true detail, one feeling, and one thing I would want my family or future self to remember.
```

## 2. Turn rough notes into a readable entry

**Use when:** You have shorthand, voice-to-text, scattered thoughts, or imperfect grammar.

```text
Turn these rough notes into a Legacy Journal entry. Preserve my voice and meaning. Do not invent details or make me sound wiser than I was.

[paste notes]
```

## 3. Record a family story

**Use when:** You remember something about a parent, grandparent, sibling, child, holiday, job, home, or family tradition.

```text
Help me preserve a family memory. Ask what happened, who was there, what I remember clearly, what may be uncertain, and why I want the family to keep it.
```

## 4. Sort out a conflict without rewriting history

**Use when:** You are angry, hurt, confused, or unsure whether your interpretation is fair.

```text
Run the Legacy Journal Truth Gate on what I tell you. Separate what happened, what I felt, what I interpreted, and what I do not know.
```

## 5. Write on a low-energy or painful day

**Use when:** You are tired, sick, overwhelmed, busy, or simply do not feel like journaling.

```text
Start a Pulse entry. I only have enough energy for three honest sentences.
```

## 6. Reconstruct a missed day honestly

**Use when:** Something important happened earlier, but you did not write it down at the time.

```text
Help me reconstruct an entry from memory. Clearly label it Reconstructed, preserve uncertainty, and do not fill gaps.
```

## 7. Leave a message for someone in the future

**Use when:** You want a child, grandchild, partner, family member, or future version of yourself to understand something.

```text
Help me write a Legacy Journal entry for someone in the future. Ask what I want them to understand, what happened that taught it to me, and what they are free to question or disagree with.
```

## 8. Preserve a mistake without turning it into self-punishment

**Use when:** You want to document what went wrong, what you own, and what should change.

```text
Help me record a mistake honestly. Do not excuse it, dramatize it, or turn it into a redemption speech. Separate what I did, what happened because of it, what I do not know, and the next repair action.
```

## 9. Create an affirmation that does not feel fake

**Use when:** You need encouragement but reject exaggerated motivational language.

```text
Create one grounded affirmation from this entry. Use only evidence already present. Move down the believability ladder until the statement is honest enough to carry.
```

## 10. Review an entry from several perspectives

**Use when:** You understand the basics and want deeper examination without changing the original entry.

```text
Run Working With the Weird Human. Give me the Careful Reader, Systems Reader, and Blunt Reader views. Keep my original entry unchanged and leave the Human Ruling for me.
```

## 11. Preserve work, building, or research progress

**Use when:** The day involved a project, experiment, repair, design, business, or long-running body of work.

```text
Help me write a Record entry about today’s work. Separate what was completed, what changed, what failed, what remains uncertain, and what the next person would need to continue from here.
```

## 12. Prepare a private entry for limited sharing

**Use when:** You want to share something with family without exposing the raw private version.

```text
Preserve my private original unchanged. Create a separate family-share version. Remove unnecessary identifying, legal, medical, financial, recovery, and third-party details. Show me what you removed and why.
```

## 13. Prepare something for public publication

**Use when:** You want to adapt a private experience into a public essay, book passage, report, or example.

```text
Preserve my private original. Create a separate public adaptation and run the Legacy Journal privacy and publication checklist. Generalize roles, dates, places, and identifying details. Do not publish or finalize anything until I approve the adaptation.
```

## 14. Ask the system what mode fits

**Use when:** You know something matters but do not know how much to write.

```text
I want to preserve something but I do not know whether it should be a Pulse, Breath, Conversation, or Record. Ask me no more than four questions, recommend the smallest mode that works, and explain the choice in one sentence.
```

## 15. Do almost nothing—and still leave a trace

**Use when:** Presence is the entire purpose.

```text
Help me write the smallest possible honest Legacy Journal entry for today. One true sentence, one point of connection, and one closing. Nothing more.
```

## Beginner rule

You do not need to use every feature.

Use the smallest part of the system that helps preserve what matters. The deeper machinery remains available when the situation earns it.
