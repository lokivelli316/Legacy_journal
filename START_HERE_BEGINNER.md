# Start Here — Beginner Guide

You do not need to understand the machinery before using Legacy Journal.

This page exists because, during a real attempt to explain the system to family, even the creator stopped and said:

> “That’s easier said than done, lmao. I barely understand it—but I do know it’s actually useful, and knowing it’s useful isn’t the same as knowing its internal mechanisms.”

Fair enough.

Legacy Journal had grown into entry modes, truth labels, privacy controls, AI rules, affirmations, review roles, templates, and publication safeguards. The system worked—but the entrance had started looking like the engine room.

So here is the dashboard.

## The whole method in four steps

1. Choose who you are writing toward.
2. Preserve one true thing.
3. Say why you wanted them to know it.
4. Save it privately unless you deliberately choose to share it.

That is enough for a complete entry.

## Your first five-minute entry

Copy this into a private note, document, notebook, or chat:

```text
Date:

I am writing toward:

One true thing from today:

Why I want them to know it:

Something I may be wrong or uncertain about:

Closing:
```

You are done when the true thing has been preserved. Length is optional.

## Choose the smallest entry that works

- **Pulse — 30 to 90 seconds:** one true sentence, one connection, one closing.
- **Breath — 3 to 5 minutes:** one moment, feeling, mistake, question, or joy.
- **Conversation — 10 to 20 minutes:** a story or lesson worth explaining.
- **Record — as long as needed:** complex work, repair, discovery, or disputed history.

When tired, choose Pulse. A short honest entry is more useful than a perfect entry that never gets written.

## Begin from a real-life situation

Not sure what to write? Start from a use case instead of a blank page:

- preserve an ordinary day;
- turn rough notes or voice-to-text into an entry;
- record a family story;
- sort out a conflict without rewriting history;
- write on a painful or low-energy day;
- reconstruct a missed day honestly;
- leave a message for a future child or grandchild;
- create one grounded affirmation that does not feel fake;
- review an entry from several perspectives.

See [Beginner Use Cases](BEGINNER_USE_CASES.md) for copy-and-paste prompts.

## Three separate decisions

**Writing is private by default.**

1. **Write privately.** Preserve the raw record.
2. **Share deliberately.** Choose a trusted reader or family archive.
3. **Publish separately.** Create a reviewed adaptation rather than exposing the private original.

Do not place raw private entries in this public repository.

## What you do not need to learn today

You do not need to understand:

- GitHub;
- Markdown;
- JSON schemas;
- every Truth Gate label;
- multi-AI review roles;
- publication metadata;
- the wider research and book ecosystem.

Those pieces are available when they become useful. They are guardrails, not an entrance exam.

## One final rule

AI may help organize the record. It may not silently become the witness.

The human author decides what happened, what it meant, what remains unknown, what stays private, and what may be published.

You do not need a tour of the laboratory. You only need to know which button does **not** release the lightning. ⚡
