# Prompt: Privacy Review

Audit the supplied public draft for information that could identify or expose a real person.

Look for:

- names, aliases, usernames, initials, and relationship clues
- exact dates, locations, schools, employers, providers, courts, or institutions
- contact details and account identifiers
- medical, legal, financial, recovery, or family details
- unique project names or phrases searchable elsewhere
- third-party messages or stories lacking consent
- image or document metadata concerns
- combinations of harmless facts that become identifying together

Return:

1. high-risk disclosures
2. moderate-risk disclosures
3. low-risk clues
4. exact recommended generalizations
5. whether the draft is safe to publish: YES / NO / NEEDS HUMAN REVIEW

Do not rewrite away the meaning. Minimize disclosure while preserving usefulness.
